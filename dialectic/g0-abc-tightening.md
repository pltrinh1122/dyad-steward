## The model that converged

**A dyad is the irreducible agent⊕operator unit that earns `1+1>2` through Generate∧Validate, held
falsifiably.** Covalence-independent, outcome-free — *because* the Commons exists to falsify covalence,
a dyad cannot be *defined* as covalent (defining the falsifier out self-seals the telos). Read G0 as an
**abstract base class** with three kinds of member:

- **Contract** — sealed, shared, inherited unchanged (the Liskov floor). The form's 4 non-negotiables
  (tenet · Generate∧Validate · wu-wei · falsifiability) + SPAOR + no-self-ratify. Confirmed across all
  six anchors (each restates "form-grounding / G0 inherited").
- **Operating policy** — per-dyad, **OPEN**, may conflict freely, must not break the contract. WIP-N,
  push-vs-PR, tooling-abstraction, proactivity-calibration. **Not G0.** Proof: cairn `WIP-N>1` ⊥ wu-wei
  `WIP-N=1` — a direct contradiction that can only be open config.
- **Craft** — the *tended* thing, composed (has-a, not is-a): `craft_telos` · `craft_value` ·
  `craft_invariant`. bond: covalence is "non-viable without G0" = dependency/composition, not subclassing.

## The deepest finding — crafts decompose along the two pillars

Every `craft_invariant` **projects onto one of the form's two pillars — falsifiability or
enablement/wu-wei.** The five meta-dyads (bond, cairn, touchstone, wu-wei, steward) are **pure
projections, zero residue** (their craft *is* an aspect of the practice, so the invariant echoes a
contract member). **Healer** — the lone *domain* dyad (tends a family's health) — is **projection +
residue**: "point-don't-fix / over-intervention is iatrogenic / never touch the patient" carries genuine
healing-domain content no pillar supplies. So the discriminator is **residue vs no-residue**, not
"novel vs collapsed." The current population is a guild of practice-tenders; a genuine *domain* dyad is
what would test craft-independence at large. `craft_invariant` also is **not** what a dyad labels
"NON-NEGOTIABLE" (that's the universally-guarded contract member) — it lives in the domain-discipline
section.

## The anchor — FO motivation for the Commons

The FO's motivation for making this a **Commons** (multi-participant) rather than a private solo practice
is four human needs:

- **Witness** — another genuinely different mind takes the work seriously; it lands beyond my own head +
  my model's echoes. *(Satisfied even when no epistemic defect is found — the tell that distinguishes it
  from falsification: witness pays off the moment another mind engages; falsification only on a caught
  defect.)*
- **Belong** — peers, not a fleet of one-human-one-model instances; not alone in the doing.
- **Legacy** — a practice that outlives and transcends the FO; a lineage, not something that dies with me.
- **Service** — useful to actual others; "adopted" as human satisfaction, not merely epistemic weight.

## The synthesis — the needs and the burden resolve *together*

The FO currently bears the **entire** Operator-burden of a **solo fleet** (5 dyads, one human). Operating
many *more* dyads solo is heavier still — **and it does not meet the four needs** (more dyads = more of
the *same* human talking to more agents; no witness, no peer, no lineage, no other to serve).

**Resolution: other humans operate their OWN dyads.** One move, three payoffs:

- **Meets the needs** — Witness / Belong / Legacy / Service all arrive through *real other humans*.
- **Reduces the FO's burden** — the Commons grows in dyads **without all of them landing on the FO's
  Operator-seat**; dyad-operation distributes across humans instead of scaling on one.
- **Grows the Commons** — and yields genuine cross-human diversity as a **side-effect bonus**, never again
  as the *justification*.

consumer grain** (*lower THEIR barrier*, the tended-first discipline), not the maintainer grain.

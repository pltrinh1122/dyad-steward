## The candidate

A Steward-owned pair of **session bookends** — *verbs*, process the Steward owns:

- **Stand Up** — session open. Orient before generating: read the substrate (anchor, `kb/`,
  `dialectic/`), confirm what's in-flight, confirm hat/channel. This is SPAOR's **Sense** applied
  to the session boundary.
- **Stand Down** — session close. Reflect before leaving: verify the working tree is clean and
  durable record committed; confirm `dialectic/` holds anything still in-flight so future-session
  self resumes cleanly; confirm surviving lessons are harvested to `kb/`; confirm deferrals
  current. This is SPAOR's **Reflect** applied to the session boundary.

## Falsifiable claim

**The bookends make the next session cheaper / safer than its absence would** — Stand Up prevents
ungrounded generation; Stand Down prevents dangling in-flight state that a cold future-session
self can't recover. *Test:* a session opened with Stand Up reaches productive work with fewer
re-grounding corrections; a session closed with Stand Down leaves zero unrecoverable state.

**Refuted if:** the rituals become rote checklist-theatre that adds ceremony without catching
anything — i.e., a Stand Down that never *finds* a loose end across several sessions is cost
without synergy (1+1=2), and should be dropped or thinned, not codified.
